# Gems

Gems is a mechanic introduced at level 100.
A new "Inlay" option will appear when enhancing gear.
Gems can be freely added and removed from gear.
A gem option will also appear in the alchemy pot for combining.

Gems can be obtained from Whaleback Island, Hades Pavilion, Gem ore, and occasional drops.

## Whaleback Island
Whaleback island is a guaranteed way to get gems in season 1, once it unlocks!
Supposedly, the progression gets quite difficult, and most F2P will not complete it until after season 2 drops.
This content is standalone, so you can continue working on it after the season.
Once it is completely cleared, it will vanish.

## Gem Simulator
There is a [simulator](https://gemsys.net/) website made by a fan, partly as a warning.
You can simulate gameplay with gems, and see how your odds actually play out.
It also has a lot of really nice, color-coded information and stats.

It also provides a cost calculator.
Long story short, it gets very inefficient to reach higher tiers for what you gain stat wise.

## Quality vs Quantity
Quantity beats Quality, Quality is a secondary factor.
Each gem provides, say, +1% speed. Combine 4, you get +2% speed. This is good, but it would be better to equipt the four gems for +4% speed, and save up to do tier 2 later.

Stat increases are linear, but cost will compound per gem.

Enhancing quality is a gamble, for T6+.
Up to tier 5 is guaranteed.
Pity will guarantee success eventually, but may be story gated.

When you fail a gamble, feeder gems may be destroyed, but the primary will not be, and will never derank.

## Sockets
Gems are slotted into sockets on gear.
It can be freely moved between gear or removed at no cost/penalty.
Gear earns its first socket at equip level 100, and further sockets at 130, 160, and 190.

## F2P / Light Spenders
You can buy more low tier gems to upgrade with, but the cost adds up.
If you wait for the story gate, it will introduce a pity floor, and it will cost potentially significantly less gems.
**Just save up until the story unlock!**

Stop at T6 or T7.
